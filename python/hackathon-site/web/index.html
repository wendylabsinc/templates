TL;DR

Big industrial sites need robots for safety, security, detection and reporting. Today's robots are mostly “glorified sensors” on fixed routes. Your challenge: using a Unitree robot dog plus edge AI, make it read real industrial instruments and report back like a teammate — robust, low-maintenance, and operator-friendly. We share a small fleet of dogs; you book hands-on deployment slots to test and demo.

The framing

Across the world's largest industrial sites — power plants, oil & gas facilities, heavy infrastructure — the same needs recur: safety, security, detection, and reporting. Inspecting equipment, spotting leaks and anomalies, reading instruments, and reporting back, around the clock, often in places that are hot, remote, or unsafe for people.

Robots should be perfect for this. But most today are glorified sensors: pricey quadrupeds walking pre-programmed routes like a camera on legs. They're disconnected from the operators who run the site, painful to set up, and fragile the moment reality differs from the script.

This hackathon is about closing that gap: moving robots from “sensor on a fixed route” to companions and enablers that work alongside operators, and that people actually want to work with.

The goal(s)

Primary: Make a robot dog navigate autonomously a defined area, understand instructions, read real industrial instruments, detect hazardous situations, and report back.

What we value:

  • Useful — does real operator work.
  • Robust — the systems stay operational.
  • Low “petting” — minimal hand-holding; it should mostly just work without an engineer per task.
  • Trainable — easy for operators to teach, adjust, and improve for their specific tasks (open ended).
  • Wanted — interacting with it feels like working with a capable teammate.

Hardware

  • Robot platform: Unitree quadruped.
  • The inspection scene: custom-made track in the premises with TBD industrial equipment.
  • Compute: Jetson on top of the Go2.
  • Fleet size: start with 1 robot — feel free to make your own fleet solution.

Software

  • WendyOS: to deploy your apps into the dogs (will be explained).
  • Bring your own: your vision/AI models and frameworks — you're free to use any stack.
